Has The Cure For AIDS Already Been Found?

 

So the International AIDS Conference in Toronto was shocked when the South African contingent showed up with an exhibit booth that displayed garlic cloves, lemons, African potatoes and beetroot.  A doctor attending the conference said the South African exhibit was “despicable.”  He added that there was “no scientific evidence showing that any of the products were effective” against HIV. 

 

But Treatment Action Campaign spokesperson Mark Heywood conceded that only 17% of people with AIDS in South Africa had received drug treatment, mostly due to the lack of affordability of drugs, and that drug resistance is a problem.  So it’s no wonder South Africans are reaching for simple and economical alternatives, as antiquated as they may seem.  But are they?

South Africa’s health minister, Dr Manto Tshabalala-Msimang, emphasizes nutrition as an "alternative" to antiretroviral medication.  Yet she is belittled and criticized.  Newspapers continue to print anti Tshabalala-Msimang propaganda that are veiled attempts at promoting the idea that HIV/AIDs is a drug deficiency.

 

While the anti-Tshabalala-Msimang campaign continues, doctors in Malawi report that home-based nutritional therapy benefited more than half of the HIV-infected children who did not receive anti-retroviral drugs.  [Acta Paediatrica 94: 222-25, 2005]  The Canadian HIV Trials Network reports that many HIV/AIDS patients do not have enough food to eat.  [HIV/AIDS Policy & Law Review 10: 22, 2005]  Food is even less food in Africa which is a continent of underdeveloped nations.  High protein nutrition is an effective approach among HIV patients in developing countries.  [Journal Advanced Nursing 54: 676-82, 2006] 


Using God To Push Anti-HIV Drugs: Example of anti-Tshabalala-Msimang propaganda campaign in South African newspapers

 

As for the doctor’s claim that lemon peel and garlic cloves are archaic - lemon peel contains quercetin [Journal American Pharmaceutical Association 40: 384-85, 1951] which is a known inhibitor of enzymes called reverse transcriptase which HIV retroviruses rely upon.  [Antiviral Research 12: 99-110, 1989] Quercetin inhibits an HIV gene product that leads to increased HIV viral replication.  [Biochemical Biophysical Research Communication 26: 308-16, 1999]  One wonders if quercetin isn’t a natural cure for HIV all by itself.

 

Garlic has been shown to inhibit all manner of viruses, ranging from herpes to polio, influenza and vaccinia (smallpox) viruses.  [Planta Medica 58: 417-23, 1992]  Garlic has been shown to be more effective than many drugs.  [Scandinavian Journal Immunology 52: 491-95, 2000]  Garlic inhibits liver enzymes (cytochrome p450) that helps to overcome resistance to anti-HIV drugs, but it has been mischaracterized and HIV patients are advised to avoid garlic because it will make their drugs work too well.  Of course, this is solved by lowering the dose of the drugs, but this would reduce the profits of the drug companies.  [American Journal Therapy 11: 262-77, 2004]

 

 

 

 


Doomed to fail: Anti HIVAIDS drugs are failing due to drug resistance.  Over-reliance upon man-made drug molecules is costing lives.

 

Above all else, protect drug patents

US politicians, working in league with pharmaceutical companies, continue their efforts to protect drug patents and resist use of generic drugs, or substitute old drugs whose patents have expired for newly patents ones.  It is obvious that those in control of AIDS funding don’t want any cheap remedies to be found. 

 

The US controls the funds used to fight AIDs and President Bush named a former chair and CEO of a major drug company as global AIDS coordinator.  The pharmaceutical industry must protect its $3.8 billion of sales of anti-retroviral drugs in the US market. In the United States, the cost of anti-retroviral drugs is generally in the range of $10,000 to $15,000 per patient annually, and people with advanced cases of AIDS may pay far more.  The raw materials for these drugs are not expensive.  Americans and insurance companies continue to pay exorbitant prices for anti-HIV drugs only because US politicians don’t object to being overcharged.

 

Far fewer receive anti-HIV drugs

It is stated by some sources that there are an estimated 5.8 million HIV-positive adults in developing countries who comprise the market for antiretrovirals.  Only about 700,000 people in developing countries were reported to be taking antiretroviral drugs at the end of 2004, or 12% of the potential market. However, a study released signed by 128 members of the faculty of Harvard University estimated that the sub-Saharan pool of HIV-infected individuals is 25 million and that only 10,000 receive antiretroviral treatment.

 

The cost of anti-HIV drugs goes beyond the cost of drugs.  It estimated that the cost of increasing antiretroviral treatment to cover 1 million people -- including testing and building the necessary health infrastructure -- would be $ 1.1 billion a year for three years, or a little more than $ 1,000 per patient annually.

 

A Harvard College report states that, because of financial pressures, patients stop taking antiretroviral drugs until they feel severely ill again. Such intermittent use can create AIDS viruses that are resistant to the drugs.  [Washington Post June 12, 2001]

 

The failed pursuit of a vaccine

The quest for an anti-HIV vaccine has been pursued since 1981 when the disease was first announced to the public.  Since then over $200 billion has been spent in developing an AIDS vaccine.  Philanthropist Bill Gates has stepped into the breach to offer $528 million to develop an AIDS vaccine.  Some 30 human AIDS vaccines have been tested in humans, but no data is available on what happened to the human guinea pigs who were given these failed vaccines.

 

Natural cures abound, but are overlooked

Are the only alternatives more expensive and problematic anti-retroviral drugs and failed vaccines?

Harold D. Foster, of the Department of Geography at the University of Victoria in British Columbia, Canada, reports that HIV infection deprives infected individuals of an essential antioxidant enzyme, glutathione peroxidase.  HIV does this by depleting the trace mineral selenium and three amino acids (cysteine, glutamine and tryptophan), which results in the major symptoms of AIDS.  According to Foster, researchers in Botswana have employed these nutrients and have achieved a 99% reversal rate of AIDS with some patients returning to work within 3 weeks.  [Medical Hypotheses 62: 549-53, 2004] 

Furthermore, blacks represent the vast majority of AIDS patients in Africa and their dark skin coloration inhibits the natural production of vitamin D from solar skin exposure.  Decreased levels of vitamin D have been found among HIV-infected patients.  [Nutrition Reviews 64: 226-33, 2006]  HIV destroys a type of white blood cell called CD4.  The progression of AIDS is measured by CD4 count.  Higher vitamin D levels are correlated with higher CD4 counts.  [Nutrition Hospital 17: 285-89, 2002]  Provision of omega-3 fish or flaxseed oil has also been shown to increase the CD4 count.  [Annals Medicine Interna 22: 323-25, 2005]

 

There are more economical and effective remedies for AIDS that do not induce viral resistance.  It’s just that the US holds control of the funds used to purchase drugs, which is the sole approach to treatment.   Millions will continue to die of HIV/AIDS simply because the drugs, as effective as they are reported to be, simply are programmed to fail due to drug resistance.  Shall we reconsider lemon peel and garlic cloves?   –Copyright 2006 Bill Sardi, Knowledge of Health, Inc.
