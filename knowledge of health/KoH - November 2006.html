    Dementia and Fish


    Disease investigators have long known that nervous system disorders like multiple sclerosis occur more frequently in inland areas away from fish. Now there is compelling data that says fish consumption signficantly reduces the onset of dementia (Alzheimer's disease). Researchers at the US Department of Agriculture Human Nutrition Research Center on Aging at Tufts University in Boston report on 899 men and women (avg age 76 years) who were free of mental decline at the beginning of the study. Out of 899 subjects, 99 developed dementia over 9 years with those subjects who consumed more omega-3 oils from fish experiencing 43% less risk. The marker used in the study was DHA, aa component of fish oil. -Annals Neurology Nov. 2006.

    They are still claiming statin drugs are beneficial.


    They're at it again, claiming statin drugs are beneficial. The latest review is a pooled analysis from 7 studies. The 7 trials involved 42,848 patients who took statin drugs for 4.3 years on average. The researchers claim statin drugs reduced heart attacks by 29% and strokes by 14%. However, these are relative numbers. There wasn't a significant reduction in coronary heart disease mortality (same number of people died). The absolute (not relative) risk reduction for a heart attack was 1.7%, meaning 60 patients would need to be treated for an average of 4.3 years to prevent 1 major coronary event. In low-risk patients, the reduction in major strokes or heart attacks would be 0.75%, with high-risk patients experiencing a 2.51% reduction. So the relative difference between low and high risk groups is over 300%, but only an absolute difference of 1.76%. The researchers speculated if 23 million Americans took statin drugs about 383,000 heart attacks and 85,800 strokes would be prevented over 5 years, but the same number of people would die, at a cost of about $40-155 billion for the medication. The whole report is found in the November 27, 2006 issue of the Archives of Internal Medicine, volume 166, pages 2307-13. Copyright 2006 Bill Sardi

    Did anybody hear about the recent vitamin E analysis?


    Did anybody hear about the recent vitamin E analysis? It showed that male users of vitamin E supplements, and no one else, achieved a blood concentration of vitamin E that conferred an 18% relative decreased risk for all causes of death, a 30% decline in risk for death due to cancer or cardiovascular disease, compared to men whose vitamin E level was very low. The population studied were males age 50-69 years in Finland who smoked cigarettes. [American Journal Clinical Nutrition 84: 1200-07, 2006] Extra-high doses of vitamin E were not required to achieve these reductions in health risks. Only about 100 milligrams of vitamin E worked and larger doses did not produce a greater effect. Dietary intake of vitamin E is about 9-11 milligrams per day and the Recommended Daily Allowance is 15 milligrams per day, which 93-96% of men and women respectively do not consume. The advantage goes only to the supplement users. [American Journal Clinical Nutrition 84: 959-60, 2006]

    90,000 deaths occur in U.S. hospitals


    While federal health authorities announce about 90,000 deaths occur in U.S. hospitals annually from infections, hospitals outside the U.S. automatically quarantine patients as they are admitted and quickly determine if they are resistant to antibiotics. If patients are antibiotic resistant, then special infection-control measures will be utilized throughout their hospital stay.

    Testing for drug-resistant forms of bacteria upon admission and weekly thereafter has been shown to reduce infection rates in intensive care units by 75% and in hospital wards by 40% and an overall reduction in drug-resistant infections by 67%. Other methods of infection control, such as hand washing, were ineffective. [Clinical Infectious Diseases 43: 971-78, 2006] Where is the Centers for Disease Control? US hospitals are allowing patients to die needlessly of avoidable infections. A more in-depth report can be found at: http://www.slate.com/id/2152118

    US hospitals are not prepared to treat fresh heart attack patients


    In recent days we have learned that……….

    ….Many US hospitals are not prepared to treat fresh heart attack patients in a timely manner. Only a third of fresh heart attack patients who need urgent re-establishment of circulation to the heart are able to receive it within the suggested 90-minute time window.

    ….After the 90-minute time window, angioplasty may offer no benefit and may slightly increase the risk of death compared to medical therapy with blood thinners and clot busters alone.

    ….And that wire artery props (called stents) that are placed into coronary arteries may often fail due to blood clots forming around the stents or re-narrowing of the artery despite the placement of the stent. Thousands of Americans are now dying annually from sudden heart attacks caused by the collapse or narrowing of the stents themselves.

    Why don't doctors prescribe vitamin pills with oral contraceptives?


    The lifestyle of a young woman who has frequent sexual contacts with different partners, who takes birth control pills and smokes cigarettes to avoid the weight gain caused by the oral contraceptives, is all too common. The risk for transmission of the human papilloma virus that causes cervical cancer increases with all of the above factors, but some women who acquire the virus never develop cancer. Researchers now think they know why.

    Pap smears reveals that women who smoke are many times more likely to exhibit high viral levels and precancerous changes in their cervical tissue than nonsmokers. [Cancer Epidemiology Biomarkers, Oct. 20, 2006 online]

    Doctors at the Karolinska Institute in Sweden, where the above link between smoking and cervical cancer was measured, claim that smoking has an enigmatic role in cervical cancer. Enigmatic means mysterious, unclear, puzzling. The role of smoking in cervical cancer is not enigmatic.

    Smoking depletes the body of folic acid and vitamin C to the point where the immune system is compromised and the papilloma virus takes hold and years later will cause cancer. An abnormal Pap smear indicates early trouble and doctors then recommend surgical removal of the tissue, all which could be avoided with an improved diet and vitamin pills.

    The Karolinska Institute in Sweden is very prestigious. Yet researchers there overlooked a report in the Journal of Infectious Disease in 2003 that the risk for papilloma virus infection is related to low vitamin C levels. [Journal Infectious Disease 188: 1508-16, 2003] More than a decade ago researchers at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine in New York reported that smoking women are more likely to have low vitamin C levels and pre-cancerous lesions of the cervix. [Cancer Detection Prevention 15: 165-70, 1991] Similar findings have been made for low levels of folic acid and papilloma virus infection. [Cancer Epidemiology Biomarkers 2: 525-30, 1993]

    There are many who oppose mandatory vaccination against the papilloma virus. There are others who claim that those who oppose such a vaccine will expose their children to infection because their children may have sexual contact with a non-vaccinated partner. Such folly. The papilloma vaccine is unproven and does not offer protection against all strains of the papilloma virus. Young women may be at the same risk for cervical cancer regardless of whether they are vaccinated or not.

    Why don't doctors prescribe vitamin pills with oral contraceptives? Vitamins offer protection against all strains of the papilloma virus. Doctors are subjecting their patients to eventual surgery or cancer by neglecting the published reports on the link between nutrition and cervical cancer. Furthermore, vaccine does not confer protection against all forms of sexually-transmitted disease (herpes, Chlamydia, others).

    Parents who accompany their daughters to the doctor's office for their first prescription for oral contraceptives are giving license to activity that can be very harmful. The provision of oral contraceptives from health clinics on college campuses encourages the spread of disease. Those who espouse sex only in context of marriage and who object to mandatory papilloma virus vaccination are now ridiculed. What convoluted thinking. -Copyright 2006 Bill Sardi, Knowledge of Health, Inc.

    American Medical Association doesn't like competition


    So the American Medical Association doesn't like competition. The AMA slammed bio-identical hormone therapy, promoted by actress Suzanne Somers. The AMA says these hormones are unregulated, pose the same health risks as prescription-drug versions, and need to undergo safety studies. The battle is between horse-urine (the Rx pharmaceutical version) and plant-based hormones (bio-identical hormones), between standard-dose hormones vs individualized hormone therapy.

    This is a turf battle supreme, the AMA versus one woman. Picking on a female like Suzanna Somers is a public relations mistake for the AMA. The AMA says pharmacies compounding bio-identical hormones aren't regulated enough. The pharmaceutical hormones came from highly regulated companies and slightly raised mortality rates for breast and other forms of cancer.

    Some natural-therapy-minded doctors have been prescribing bio-identical hormones for 20 years. The problem with hormone therapy has always been dosing. Women as they approach menopause are a moving target --- their naturally-produced ovarian hormone levels are slowly declining.

    A standard dose for all females was the rule until it was found they increase mortality rates. Modern medicine has left menopausal women in a lurch. Without hormone therapy their bones wither, their mood declines and many are inappropriately placed on anti-depressant drugs, and skin/vaginal/eye dryness is pervasive. For women who don't want to go to doctors for bio-identical hormones, 1-2 tablespoons of flaxseed meal added to yogurt or whole grain cereal does wonders. Flaxseed contains lignans, weak estrogen-like molecules. -Copyright 2006 Bill Sardi, Knowledge of Health, Inc.

    Are doctors "lapdogs" to drug companies


    Drugs aren't intended to cure and doctors are "lapdogs" to drug companies, say two outspoken critics of the medical care industry.

    Doctors must not be 'lapdogs' to drug companies, says Adriane Fugh-Berman, Associate Professor in Complementary and Alternative Medicine at Georgetown University.
    Speaking out in the British Medical Journal,

    Dr. Fugh-Berman said "Drug representatives are paid to be nice to us as long as we cooperate, sustaining market share of targeted drugs, and limiting our continuing medical education lectures to messages that increase drug sales."

    She claims the drug industry is happy to play the 'genial uncle' until physicians want to discuss subjects that are off-limits, such as the benefits of diet or exercise, or the relationship between medicine and pharmaceutical companies.

    At a medical meeting, Dr. Fugh-Berman caused pharmaceutical company representatives to pack up their exhibits and walk out after she delivered a speech to doctors on how drug companies conduct psychological profiling and monitoring of physicians, including prescription tracking.

    In Britain, Ken Eckersley, representing The Campaign for the Effective Prevention & Treatment of Addiction, speaking out in a report entitled "Are taxpayer-paid pharmaceutical drugs worth dying for?" said "more and more prescribed drugs are curing fewer and fewer illnesses, because of the current psycho-pharmaceutical policy of disease and addiction 'management' instead of the seeking of actual cures."

    Eckersley added that "psycho-pharm marketing chiefs have recognized that every time a patient is cured of his illness or an addict is cured of his habit, pharmaceutical companies loses a customer for the drug they have been supplying. As a result, it was decided that 'treatment' which brings 'relief' or "support" or which 'manages' the illness or habit essentially throughout the lifetime of the individual, could be vastly more profitable than getting rid of the illness or addiction by curing it."

    He went on to point out that "this ensures the numbers of patients rise year by year, so that we continuously need more hospital premises, more equipment, more staff and of course more taxpayer paid drugs - the proof of which is plain for all to see." -Copyright 2006 Bill Sardi

    Genes switches


    The Food & Drug Administration has approved Zolinza, a drug to treat T-cell lymphoma. Zolinza (vorinostat) is a new class of drug that switches off genes associated with the development of cancer. Other drugs similar to Zolinza are soon to be approved.

    Zolinza is different from the gene-targeted drugs like Erbituz and Gleevec that switch just a few genes. Zolinza switches a multitude of genes. [The Economist Oct. 12, 2006] Its approval as an “orphan drug” to treat a less common form of cancer appears to be part of an effort to rescue a drug giant that is currently on shaky financial ground. [Drug News & Perspectives 19: 2006]

    According to the National Cancer Institute, vorinostat (Zolinza) produces side effects, such as diarrhea (51%), fatigue (51%), nausea (43%), and anorexia (27%), fatigue (7%), and pulmonary embolism (5.4%). Vorinostat also causes chemical abnormalities such as high cholesterol (66%), high triglycerides (66%), and high blood sugar (64%) as well as anemia (54%) low white blood cell count (24%), and a decline in blood platelets (42%). It is difficult to ascertain how this drug obtained FDA approval.

    Nature has its master gene switcher – resveratrol, which has been shown to favorably trip gene-controlled protein making. [Cancer Biology Therapy 3:882-8, 2004] Dr. John Pezzuto, the pioneering researcher who uncovered resveratrol’s anti-cancer properties, says resveratrol is a “whiff that induces a biologically specific tsunami,” referring to its broad ability to favorably switch thousands of genes. [Cancer Biology Therapy 3:889-90, 2004] In the 35 years of the war on cancer, there has yet to arise a drug that would rival resveratrol anti-cancer properties. How many more cancer deaths before resveratrol is employed by oncologists?
