How a Subscriber Saved $16,000 in Dental Work by Heading to Mexico. But It Took Him Two Tries.
Gary North

January 21, 2009

One way that Americans are escaping the high cost of medical care is to go to third world countries for elective surgery.

This applies to expensive dental work.

Here, Hank tells how he beat the system.

* * * * * * * * *

Last March, I awoke to a bad tooth ache. I only see the dentist when pain is the motivator. My Asheville, N.C. dentist took a look and informed me that I needed a crown. No problem, I thought, as the last crown I had back in the mid-nineties cost me $350. No more. He charged me $1,032 for a PFM or porcelain fused to metal crown. One month later, I chomp down on a solid, crispy apple and a molar cracks. Back to the dentist. Yup, another crown. Another $1,032. It occurred to me that this process will continue and every time I need to have a tooth done it will cost me a grand. Not good. I decide to get proactive with this and ask the dentist for an estimate on rehabbing all my teeth as a means to compare. He wants to do nine crowns on the lowers and nine veneers on the uppers. This works out to roughly a grand per tooth or $18,000. I'm not buying this. Dentists like doctors in the U.S. charge way too much. I will shop around. For the past twenty years, I have been a sometimes subscriber to International Living. I went to their website and subscribed anew. I searched for medical tourism and found several articles on dentists in Mexico. I purposed to fly down this past July and scout out beach property and dentists. Instead of flying direct to Merida, I fly into Cancun, which is one half the cost. I then take an express bus to Merida and stay at the Suites Del Sol for $38 per night. http://suitesdelsol.com

I call a dentist who was recommended. He does a look-see in my mouth and gives me three options, none of which I really like. I get the feeling the guy is out for the money and wants to sell me more than I need. It's Mexico after all. I do more real estate looking than dentist looking and the week runs out and I fly home. Once home, I do more internet searching for dentists in earnest. I locate a dentist and start emailing him. I like the guy's responses to my email. His website is very well done. We exchange lots of email. I am getting a very good feeling about this fellow. I schedule another trip down. I arrive last September for a stay of two weeks. Dr. Javier Camara has his first look at my teeth and tells me that he would do nine crowns on the lowers and fill in the pits in my uppers with resin and then whiten my uppers. Total cost: $2350. I say, do it. With that, in walks two women who proceed to prepare my teeth. In one hour and fifteen minutes the one gal had ground down nine of my lower teeth prepping them for the crowns. She then made the temporaries and cemented them in. Next, another gal came in and filled in all my pitted upper teeth with resin. She then whitened the uppers as well. That took about an hour. I came back in seven days to have my permanent crowns fitted. Imagine, nine crowns done in seven days! They all fit but one. Dr. Camara took a new impression of the one tooth and then decided to do an additional crown on an upper tooth. He told the lab to expedite and three days later at no extra charge the crowns were ready. Back I went to the office. The remaining crowns were fitted. I payed my final installment all in cash and home I went. Dr. Camara, who by the way, speaks fluent English, practiced dentistry in Houston TX. He was in a partnership with several other dentists. He was there for a number of years but did not like the fact that his partners tried to sell their patients the most expensive procedures even when they did not need it. He finally quit and returned to his native Mexico in Merida. He is a lovely gentleman and a family man with three children. He is in his early fifties. He is extremely competent. I made the right decision and glad I did as it was no big deal at $2350. Eighteen grand is something else. I have not had medical or dental insurance for the past ten years. I don't regret that now. If I need extensive medical or further dental work I will seek it outside the U.S. The medical services in many foreign countries including Mexico are excellent and can be had for one fifth the cost in America. Dr. Camara's website: http;//incidentaltourist.com or google dr. javier camara I stayed in the town of Progreso which is on the beach north of Merida about a 40 minute bus ride from Merida. I stayed at the Nicte-Ha apartments. very nice at $38 with kitchenette. email: reservaciones_nicteha@hotmail.com Amigos Bar and Grill in Progreso on Calle 78 between 25 Y 27 is run by a Canadian couple. This is a place to meet expats mostly Canadians and Americans. Good food too! Also, Ramona Robinson is an honest lady dealing in real estate sales and rentals. her website: http://flamingocoast.com.mx I walked everywhere both day and night in Merida, a city of one million and Progreso, much smaller. I felt safe all the time. At 64, with short gray hair, people, including teens in Mexico, show respect if you are humble. Outside of Progreso is one place I would consider living in retirement. It is safe, reasonably priced and the locals are friendly especially if you attempt to speak Spanish. Only downside I saw was the trash scattered most everywhere. There is very little awareness of picking up after you make a mess.
